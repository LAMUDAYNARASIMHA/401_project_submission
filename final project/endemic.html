Endemic Species

Endemic species are those found in a specific geographic area and nowhere else in the world. Here are some examples of unique endemic species.

Aye-aye

The Aye-aye is a lemur found only in Madagascar. It is known for its unique method of finding food, tapping on trees to locate insects.

Kiwi

The Kiwi is a flightless bird endemic to New Zealand. It is the national symbol of the country and known for its unique characteristics like a long beak with nostrils at the end.

Japanese Macaque

Also known as the snow monkey, the Japanese Macaque is endemic to Japan. It is famous for bathing in hot springs during the winter.

Javan Rhinoceros

The Javan Rhinoceros is critically endangered and found only in Ujung Kulon National Park in Indonesia. It is one of the rarest large mammals in the world.

Komodo Dragon

The Komodo Dragon is the largest living species of lizard, found only on a few Indonesian islands. They are apex predators known for their size and strength.
